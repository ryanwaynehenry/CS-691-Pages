Downtown Art Walk

Self-guided route • Live demonstrations • Artist meet & greet

BENIGN

Date: Saturday, April 18  •  Check-in opens: 9:30 AM at Central Plaza  •  Free admission

Route & Schedule

9:30 AM
Check-In & Welcome
Central Plaza — Main Fountain
Collect your wristband and printed route map. Staff will give a brief orientation, highlight accessible routing options, and note any weather changes to the outdoor segments.
10:00 AM
Guided Exhibit Walk-Through
Riverfront Gallery — 14 Harbor Blvd
45-minute docent-led tour of the Urban Rhythm exhibition — 30 works exploring city life through mixed media, documentary photography, and sculptural installation. Arrive any time between 10:00 and 11:00 AM; tours depart every 15 minutes.
11:15 AM
Open Studio Visits
Foundry Building — 8 Mill Street, Studios 2 & 5
Watch two working artists mid-process and ask them questions directly. No ticketing required — walk in at any point during the 11:15 – 12:15 PM window.
12:30 PM
Live Mural Demonstration
Maple Alley — off Grove Street
Mural artist Dara Santos continues work on a new 20-foot commission in real time. Watch from the marked viewing area and chat with the artist during breaks. Expected duration: approximately 90 minutes.
2:00 PM
Artist Q&A Panel
Studio Hall — 3rd Floor, Room 301
Three featured artists discuss their practice, this year's theme, and how public art shapes neighborhood identity. Moderated Q&A follows the presentations. Seating is limited; arrive a few minutes early.
3:30 PM
Closing Reception
Central Plaza — Covered Pavilion
Light refreshments provided. Browse a small outdoor market of artist prints and handmade goods. Extra route maps available for self-guided return visits throughout the week.

Featured Artists

DS
Dara Santos
Mural / Large-Scale

Known for layered street murals that map neighborhood histories through color fields and found imagery. This is Dara's third consecutive year in the Art Walk.

RK
Remy Kowalczyk
Photography / Mixed Media

Documentary photographer whose Urban Rhythm series examines transit workers and the overlooked labor of keeping a city running.

TN
Taryn Nguyen
Sculpture / Installation

Creates site-responsive installations from reclaimed materials. This year's piece, Threshold, occupies the Riverfront Gallery entrance hall.

What to Bring

  • Comfortable shoes — approx. 1.5-mile total walking route
  • Water bottle (refill stations at Plaza and Gallery)
  • Sun protection for the Maple Alley outdoor segment
  • Camera or phone (flash off inside gallery spaces)

Practical Notes

  • Route signs posted with a teal arrow at each corner
  • Accessible routing available — ask a greeter for the Level A map
  • Free bike parking at Central Plaza and outside the Foundry Building
  • Rain plan: indoor-only route available if weather requires it
